I am Kevin Wierman, an experimental particle physicist, a coder, and a brewer.

Physics

Professionally, I am a physicist. I earned my doctorate at the University of North Carolina in 2016 and I am continuing to pursue my passion for physics beyond the standard model.

Most of my work has thematically revolved around the field of neutrino physics. My most previous work was with the KATRIN collaboration. Currently, I am looking into working with Liquid Argon Time Projection Chambers.

Coding

While by day I am a physicist, by night I code.

Since physics and coding are intractable, my goal is to bring more sophisticated techniques into the physics community so that we may produce industry grade technologies during the source of our research. Recently, I have worked with the Center for Open Science to develop the Open Science Framework.

Brewing

When I'm not working, coding or climbing, I make beer. As I take the same approach here as I do with all other things in life, I try to incorporate my scientific education into the process of making beer. Expect to see more posts here in the future as I move into the second phase of my brewing expansion.
